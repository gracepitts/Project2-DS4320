# Headline
Improving Clinical Trial Success Rates

## Hook

Bringing a new drug to the market is incredibly difficult. Nearly 90% of clinical trials fail, costing time, money, and delaying life-saving treatments.
## Problem Statement
Clinical trials are a critical step in developing new treatments, but they are expensive, time-consuming, and highly risky. Despite advances in medicine, most trials do not successfully lead to approved drugs due to issues like lack of effectiveness, safety concerns, or poor study design. Currently, it is difficult for researchers and organizations to identify which trials are more likely to succeed early on. This project focuses on understanding patterns in clinical trial data, such as trial phase, study type, and sponsor characteristics, to better identify factors associated with successful outcomes.

## Solution Description
This project uses data from ClinicalTrials.gov to analyze trends across thousands of clinical trials and identify key factors linked to success. By examining characteristics like trial phase, organization type, and intervention type, the analysis highlights patterns that can help researchers design better trials and allocate resources more effectively. The goal is to provide simple, data-driven insights that support decision-making and improve the chances of successful drug development.
